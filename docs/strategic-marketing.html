TRI Google Ads Optimization & Campaign Strategy Grad Capstone Project (Fall 2025 - Present)

For my client project with the Trauma Resource Institute (TRI), I am leading the development and optimization of Google Ads campaigns to improve lead generation and maximize the impact of the Google Ads grant.

This work involves auditing historical campaign performance, restructuring campaigns based on user intent, and aligning keywords, ad copy, and landing pages to drive higher-quality conversions.

Skills Demonstrated:

  • Search Campaign Strategy: Designed intent-based campaign structure (brand vs. non-brand, segmented by program offerings like CRM and TRM).

  • Keyword & Match Type Optimization: Evaluated performance at the keyword–match level to improve relevance, CTR, and conversion rate.

  • Conversion-Focused Thinking: Focused on optimizing for meaningful actions (training sign-ups, donations, and inquiries), not just traffic.

  • Data Analysis & Reporting: Analyzed search term data, user behavior, and campaign metrics to guide weekly optimization decisions.

  • Cross-Channel Alignment: Ensured consistency between ad messaging and landing page experience to improve conversion performance.

IBM6510 Data Analysis Project: Exploratory Data & Insights (Fall 2024)

As part of my first semester in graduate school, I led the sourcing and development of a dataset-driven analysis project using R and Quarto. I independently identified and curated a real-world animal shelter dataset, then collaborated with my team to clean, analyze, and interpret the data to uncover meaningful insights.

This project focused on understanding the factors that influence animal adoption outcomes. Through data preparation, feature engineering, and statistical analysis, I examined how variables such as breed type, intake condition, age, and intake source impact the likelihood of adoption.

The analysis included exploratory data analysis, correlation analysis, and logistic regression modeling. During modeling, I identified multicollinearity issues across predictors, which informed how results were interpreted and highlighted limitations in statistical significance despite meaningful variable relationships.

Skills Demonstrated:

  • Data Sourcing & Preparation: Identified and evaluated an external dataset, then cleaned and structured it using R (tidyverse, janitor, rio).
  • Feature Engineering: Created binary indicators for breed categories, intake conditions, and adoption outcomes to support modeling.
  • Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA): Analyzed trends in adoption rates across intake type, age, and breed classifications.
  • Statistical Modeling: Built logistic regression models to evaluate factors influencing adoption likelihood.
  • Analytical Thinking: Diagnosed multicollinearity and interpreted model limitations in a real-world dataset.
  • Data Visualization: Used correlation matrices and plots to communicate relationships between key variables.
  • Reproducible Reporting: Developed a Quarto-based report integrating code, outputs, and narrative insights.
